Two tools, one promise: the exact size, with nothing uploaded

Whether a portal demands a photo "under 50 KB" or a consulate wants a precise 35 × 45 mm passport photo at the right pixel size, the frustration is the same — vague tools that get you "close" and applications rejected for being a few kilobytes or pixels off. Both tools here aim for the exact target, run entirely in your browser, and add no watermark.

Passport & visa photo specifications

Photo rules differ by country and by document, and they're updated from time to time. Below are the specs this tool currently supports, gathered from official and authoritative sources. Always double-check the current requirement on the official portal before you submit.

United States (2 × 2 inch)

US passport and visa photos are square: 2 × 2 inches (51 × 51 mm), a digital file of 600 × 600 to 1200 × 1200 pixels, on a plain white or off-white background. The head should fill roughly 50–69% of the height. Passport renewal uploads accept 54 KB to 10 MB, while visa (DS-160) and the Diversity Visa lottery require 240 KB or smaller.

Schengen / EU visa (35 × 45 mm)

The Schengen standard is 35 × 45 mm (about 413 × 531 pixels at 300 DPI), head 32–36 mm, often on a light grey background. Online submissions through VFS-style portals typically want a JPEG comfortably under a few megabytes; keeping it under ~500 KB is safe.

United Kingdom, India, Canada

The UK uses 35 × 45 mm, with digital uploads of at least 600 × 750 pixels and 50 KB–10 MB. India's e-Visa wants a square JPEG between 350 × 350 and 1000 × 1000 pixels, 10–300 KB, white background, no glasses. Canada is unusual at 50 × 70 mm for passports (printed photos must be taken by a commercial photographer), with online renewal accepting roughly 420 × 540 pixels and up.

Frequently asked questions

Does this guarantee my photo will be accepted?
No. It gets the size, crop, format and file size right, which are the most common rejection reasons — but background, lighting, expression and head position are up to you, and final acceptance is always the authority's decision.
Does it change my background to white?
No. It does not edit your background. Take the photo against a plain, evenly lit wall in the colour your document requires.
Are my files uploaded anywhere?
No. All image processing, photo cropping and data conversion happen locally in your browser.
Why is the file-size limit different for passport vs visa?
Different government portals set different limits for the same country. Pick the exact document type so the right limit is applied.
Which data formats can I convert between?
JSON, YAML, XML and CSV, in any direction. CSV works best with a list of flat records; deeply nested data converts most cleanly between JSON, YAML and XML.
